The Story of elegant problem solving

Got blocked on a simple problem & Solved a problem in an elegant way???

In both cases:

I can't think of only one challenge, but what I do when i stand infront of a challange depends of the challenge itself. For example, if I cannot see the solution easily (speaking about code, but also in real life) I will write and make sure that I can see the problem and the goal clearly in front of me.

I will use Pseudocode or just a paper and pen to draw myself to the solution. This guides me towards the right direction, as I can see the steps I need to take on paper/pseudocode, before I'll start working on the real deal.

Another problem solving skill I'm very comfortable with is "trying something". So, after I have a clear understanding of the challenge, I will create a rough plan with a rough structure.

I'll try different paths to find a solution. On the way I'll always ask my great friend "Duck Duck" Go : ) and look for answers on https://www.w3schools.com/ thefreecodecamp, developer.mozilla...

When this doesn't help, I'll start talking to myself : ) or to my dog. This is not a sign of insanity, but smart problem solving. I'll talk to try to find understanding in the actual question I have. Furthermore, I reflect on the way in hope to find a solution in my own words.

I also enjoy "Reading error messages" which provide me with a great step by step guidance. It is so nice to work that way, as I can celebrate the success on a step by step basis.

"Console.log" is also a great support.

And when all these options don't help, I'll ask my amazing peers for help - and I have to say, that they are always extremely helpful.

Sometimes, I'll also ask my mentor for help. But that's the last resource, as I know that she's super busy : )

Yes, I do like to reflect a lot (especially on paper) - That's how I make sense of the world & Code & myself.
